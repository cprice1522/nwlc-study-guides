Question 90

You are looking at two transformers on two different poles. Transformer A has a dropout cutout mounted above it on the pole. Transformer B has no cutout above it but has a red indicator light visible on the case. Which one is the CSP and which is the conventional transformer?

Question 91

A homeowner tells you they have a 240V clothes dryer and 120V outlets in their house, both fed from the same transformer. What type of secondary service does this transformer provide, and what makes it possible to get both voltages from one transformer?

🔍 One secondary winding can supply two different voltages if it has a center tap
Question 92

On a single-phase transformer you see secondary bushings labeled X1, X2, and X3. A customer is asking where to connect for 240V and where to connect for 120V. Which terminals give you each voltage?

🔍 X2 is the center-tap neutral. X1 and X3 are the two hot legs.

🔍 Higher kVA means the transformer can handle more load

🔍 The kVA rating is the maximum the transformer is designed to deliver continuously
