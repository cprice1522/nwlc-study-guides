Quick Reference — Key Terms & Concepts

The Nameplate — What's On It

ItemWhat It Means
Voltage RatingsPrimary and secondary voltages. Primary coil rating is always the SMALLER of the two primary voltages listed.
kVA RatingApparent power capacity (wattage rating). Average home uses ~6,000W so a 10 kVA transformer is typical.
Serial NumberUnique to every transformer — used for inventory and tracking throughout the transformer's life.
PolarityDirection of current flow. Additive = X1 on the RIGHT. Subtractive = X1 on the LEFT (facing the transformer).
ImpedanceTotal opposition to current flow. If transformer is 98% efficient, impedance = 2%.
SchematicShows primary and secondary coils and their relationship.
WeightTotal weight including oil — used for rigging calculations when setting or removing a transformer.

Nameplate Location

  • Overhead transformer: On or near the lower mounting bracket. Primary voltage printed FIRST.
  • Underground/padmount transformer: Inside the door or on the face. Primary voltage printed LAST.

When to Check the Nameplate (Before Installation)

  • Ensure the desired primary voltage is correct
  • Ensure the desired secondary voltage is correct
  • Ensure impedances are within +/- 7.5% if banking or paralleling
  • Determine the weight for rigging
⚠ Always verify secondary voltage with an approved voltmeter before connecting the customer's load.

Three Primary Voltage Designation Types

TypeNameplate ExampleBushingsConnectionNotes
Phase-to-Phase Only12,0002Between two phases onlySingle number. If connected P-G: radio interference and corona problems.
Phase-to-Phase OR Phase-to-Ground7,200/12,470Y2Either way — most versatileSmaller number = coil rating. Larger number = system voltage. "Y" = connect wye on larger voltage system.
Phase-to-Ground Only12,470GrdY/7,2001Phase-to-ground onlySystem voltage listed FIRST. "Grd" = neutral end MUST be grounded. Reduced insulation at neutral end.

The Key Rule for Reading Primary Voltage Ratings

The smaller number (whether printed first or last) is ALWAYS the voltage impressed on the primary coil. The larger number is the system voltage (read phase-to-phase).

  • 7,200/12,470Y → Primary coil = 7,200V | System = 12,470V
  • 19,920/34,500Y → Primary coil = 19,920V | System = 34,500V
  • 24,900GrdY/14,400 → System = 24,900V | Primary coil = 14,400V

Dual-Rated Transformers

  • Has two primary coil ratings — installed on systems of differing voltages
  • Switched by an external no-load switch on the side of the case
  • Often marked "DUAL" on the case
  • Switch changes the primary coil turns ratio
⚠ MUST be operated ONLY when the transformer is DE-ENERGIZED

Tap Changers

  • Purpose: Provide the same secondary voltage at different locations where primary voltage varies
  • Connected to the primary winding — is a no-load manual switch
  • Each tap raises or lowers secondary voltage by 2.5% or 4.5%
  • Raised tap = fewer primary turns → maintains secondary voltage as primary drops

Transformer Testing — Three Types

TestToolWhat It ChecksKey Rule
ContinuityMultimeterCoil integrity — open or shorted windingsTone SHOULD be heard H1–H2 and between X bushings. NO tone should be heard H to X — if tone exists = defective.
Insulation (Megger)MegohmmeterInsulation damageReading between any primary and secondary bushing should be INFINITY. Remove ALL connections including X2 ground before testing.
Turns Ratio (TTR)Turns Ratio TesterTurns ratio accuracy and shorts between turnsConnect LOW input voltage to HIGH voltage winding. Does NOT reveal insulation weakness.
⚠ TTR Safety: Always connect low-input voltage to the HIGH-voltage winding. If applied to the low-voltage winding, dangerous high voltage will appear on the primary bushings.

TLC Meter (Transformer Last Check)

  • Made by Hastings — TLC = Transformer Last Check
  • Verifies no shorted or open windings prior to energizing
  • Two leads connected between bushings, press button
  • Three results: Pass, Open, or Shorted
